Introduction



Platformer games have established themselves as a cornerstone of the video game industry, captivating players of all ages with their timeless charm and engaging gameplay mechanics. Characterized by their emphasis on navigating various obstacles and environments by jumping and running, platformer games offer players a unique blend of precision and agility. From classic titles like Super Mario Bros. to modern masterpieces like Celeste, platformers have continuously pushed the boundaries of creativity and innovation in gaming. With their intuitive controls and diverse level designs, platformer games have cemented their status as a beloved genre, inspiring countless developers and leaving an indelible mark on the gaming landscape.

The Early Days



The roots of platform games can be traced back to the 1980s and 1990s with titles like "Donkey Kong" and "Super Mario Bros." These games laid the foundation for the genre, introducing players to the concept of navigating characters through levels filled with obstacles and enemies. "Super Mario Bros." in particular set the standard with its precise controls and iconic design.


In 1991, "Sonic the Hedgehog" brought a new dimension to platforming with its focus on speed and momentum, further solidifying the genre's popularity.


Today, platform games continue to evolve, with franchises like "Super Mario," "Rayman," and "Celeste" delivering innovative experiences while staying true to the genre's roots.

The Rise of the PLATFORM Genre



One significant moment in the rise of platform games came with the release of "Super Mario 64" in 1996. This groundbreaking title revolutionized the genre by introducing players to three-dimensional platforming for the first time. With its innovative level design, dynamic camera system, and expansive worlds, "Super Mario 64" set a new standard for what platform games could achieve.


Similarly, "Rayman Origins" (2011) and "Rayman Legends" (2013) revitalized the traditional 2D platformer genre with their stunning visuals, tight controls, and imaginative level design. These titles proved that there was still plenty of innovation and excitement to be found in classic platforming gameplay.


Today, platform games continue to thrive, with titles like "Super Mario Odyssey," "Hollow Knight," and "Ori and the Will of the Wisps" captivating players with their inventive mechanics and captivating worlds. With each new release pushing the boundaries of creativity and gameplay, platform games remain a cornerstone of the gaming industry, captivating players of all ages with their timeless appeal.

The PLATFORM Time Line



1985 - Super Mario Bros.

The iconic side-scrolling platformer that defined the genre.

1996 - Super Mario 64

Revolutionary 3D platformer that redefined the genre.

2002 - Metroid Prime

First-person adventure platformer set in a rich sci-fi universe.

2017 - Hollow Knight

A beautifully crafted 2D action-platformer with a deep and atmospheric world.

2020 - Ori and the Will of the Wisps

An emotionally resonant platform-adventure with breathtaking visuals and tight gameplay.
